About

Matt Genovese

Matt Genovese founded Door64 in 2007 with a goal of serving the high-tech demographic in Austin, Texas through targeted initiatives and events. Door64 has produced over 100 events from networking happy hours, job fairs, conferences, and other targeted engagements tied to specific results that directly benefit the Austin-area technology community. Countless technology professionals have found jobs, employers found their critical tech hires, companies found new business leads, and new employers arrived and grew in Austin through this unique and focused approach to community engagement.

I have a background in semiconductors (from chip design to manufacturing), as well as software development and web design. I love to learn and teach. I maintain close ties to academia, and sit on the Computer Engineering Advisory board at the Rochester Institute of Technology. I have also helped to develop a graduate-level course for engineers at the University of Texas at Austin.
